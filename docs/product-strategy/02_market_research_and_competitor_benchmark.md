# 02 - Market Research and Competitor Benchmark

## Scope and method

This benchmark was refreshed on April 4, 2026 using live official and competitor sources.

The goal is not to produce a complete market map. The goal is to identify:

- table stakes for a serious exam-prep product
- where official ecosystems already solve part of the learner problem
- where third-party products create perceived value
- where this platform can differentiate most effectively as an OET-first product

## Official exam ecosystems

### OET official ecosystem

Verified from OET official sources:

- OET is built specifically for healthcare professionals and supports 12 healthcare professions.
- Official sample tests exist for paper and computer/home formats.
- OET preparation materials include profession-specific courses, free and paid resources, videos, guides, sample tests, and preparation-partner support.
- OET Writing and Speaking are assessed against explicit criteria rather than only a single holistic label.
- OET reports per-sub-test scaled scores and letter grades, and official sources state that OET does not report an overall score or overall grade.

What this means for product strategy:

- OET candidates already have official sample access.
- A third-party platform cannot win by offering only generic practice questions.
- The winning layer is better guided improvement, profession-specific productive-skill coaching, stronger readiness interpretation, and premium review trust.

### IELTS official ecosystem

Verified from IELTS official sources:

- IELTS offers official sample tests, courses, apps, webinars, books, articles, and writing-marking videos.
- IELTS Writing uses explicit public criteria: Task Achievement or Task Response, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, and Grammatical Range and Accuracy.
- Task 1 and Task 2 are weighted differently, and Task 2 carries more weight.
- IELTS officially supports both Academic and General Training writing patterns.

What this means for product strategy:

- IELTS users already have strong official study infrastructure.
- A strong IELTS product needs task-aware reporting, not just general essay scoring.
- Shared four-skill architecture is reusable, but writing and speaking explanations must become band-logic aware.

### PTE official ecosystem

Verified from Pearson PTE official sources:

- PTE Academic is a computer-based English test covering Listening, Reading, Speaking, and Writing.
- Pearson positions Official Scored Practice Tests as the best indication of readiness and says they replicate test format and timing.
- Pearson Smart Prep is the official portal for free and premium preparation resources.
- Pearson also offers a Question Bank, Guided Practice Tests, study plans, and targeted skill resources.
- Official results are typically available within 48 hours and may take up to 5 days.
- PTE score reports include overall score plus communicative-skill breakdown.

What this means for product strategy:

- PTE readiness is tightly tied to computer-based speed, question-type familiarity, and fast score feedback.
- A credible PTE product must lean heavily into question-type drills, timing simulation, AI scoring, and rapid analytics.
- Reusing the current OET UX without a PTE-native drill engine would underperform the market.

## Third-party competitor snapshots

### Swoosh English (OET)

Observed positioning and features:

- self-study video courses across all four skills
- mock exam sets designed to replicate the real exam
- corrected-letter banks and model-answer banks
- live group classes across reading, listening, speaking, and writing
- recurring writing correction classes
- speaking mock exam small-group classes with expert feedback
- always-on community support via Telegram

Interpretation:

Swoosh sells high-touch support plus repeated productive-skill feedback. That reinforces the need for this platform to deepen expert review operations, compare-attempt analytics, and profession-specific remediation rather than staying at summary-score level.

### E2Language (IELTS)

Observed positioning and features:

- a low-friction IELTS short course
- a fully guided mock test with answers and explanations
- score calculator and strengths/weaknesses framing
- live classes, practice tests, and video lessons
- free-trial packaging to lower entry friction
- strong learner messaging around test confidence and fast improvement

Interpretation:

E2 competes on guided study structure, teacher authority, and quick perceived value. For IELTS, this platform needs sharper onboarding, clear task-specific readiness, and packaging that makes the move from free/trial to paid feel justified.

### APEUni (PTE)

Observed positioning and features:

- AI scoring across many PTE Speaking and Writing question types
- AI study plan and AI score-report analysis
- cross-device record sync
- app-first practice posture
- explicit question-type coverage for rapid drills

Interpretation:

APEUni shows what learners expect from a PTE product: fast AI feedback, question-type specificity, mobile convenience, and computer-exam rhythm. That confirms PTE should be treated as an AI-heavy engine program, not a thin exam-family toggle.

## Table stakes

A serious product in this category now needs all of the following:

1. Sample-test level realism or better.
2. Fast explanation of strengths and weaknesses after each attempt.
3. Clear Writing and Speaking improvement guidance.
4. Mock-test availability and score interpretation.
5. Mobile-friendly access to short practice loops.
6. Visible trust signals around scoring and feedback quality.

## Differentiators available to this platform

The current product can differentiate most credibly in these areas:

1. OET profession-specific productive-skill depth.
2. One platform spanning learner, expert, and admin operations.
3. Premium human-review routing inside the same practice system.
4. Readiness, study-plan, and compare-attempt loops built around action rather than content browsing.
5. A reusable shared core that can support IELTS without diluting OET.

## Whitespace opportunities

### Highest-value whitespace

1. OET-specific readiness blockers tied to profession and productive-skill evidence.
2. Better AI trust framing: confidence bands, provenance labels, escalation triggers, and explicit non-official wording.
3. Rich compare-attempt analytics for Writing and Speaking.
4. Admin-visible content provenance and QA performance before broad multi-exam authoring.
5. IELTS task-family-aware reporting that feels more operational than generic essay feedback.

### Important negative whitespace

Areas where the platform should avoid shallow expansion:

- superficial PTE support without question-type depth
- generic IELTS pages that ignore Academic vs General differences
- desktop/mobile polish work that outruns monetization and learner-loop quality

## OET-specific opportunity gaps

OET is where this platform can be best-in-class if it deepens the right things:

- profession-aware writing coaching
- speaking role-play and transcript loops grounded in clinical communication
- premium expert SLA and quality visibility
- mock-report to remediation pathways
- explicit readiness blockers for productive skills
- trusted explanation of why an AI score should or should not be escalated

## Strategic takeaway

Official ecosystems already provide access, sample material, and test-familiarization.

Third-party winners add one or more of the following:

- human coaching
- mock realism with feedback
- AI speed and repetition
- better guidance on what to do next

This platform should not try to out-official the official products. It should out-operate them by combining:

- stronger practice loops
- better trust architecture
- premium review operations
- profession-specific OET depth
- shared-core reuse for IELTS
