Answer Key with Rationale
Diarrhea and Dehydration in Children – Part A
Questions 1–7
Question 1.   Answer: A
Rationale: Text A classifies diarrhea as acute (fewer than 14 days), persistent (14 days or more but fewer 
than 30 days), and chronic (beyond 30 days).
Question 2.   Answer: D
Rationale: Text D presents a five-step protocol covering initial assessment, dehydration classification, 
rehydration plan, nutritional support, and discharge planning.
Question 3.   Answer: C
Rationale: Text C table: "Altered consciousness or lethargy" is a sign of severe dehydration; 
recommended action is "Commence intravenous fluid resuscitation".
Question 4.   Answer: B
Rationale: Text B: "Rotavirus vaccination has demonstrated effectiveness in reducing the incidence of 
severe rotavirus diarrhea in children."
Question 5.   Answer: D
Rationale: Text D, Step 4: "Feeding should not be withheld during diarrhea."
Question 6.   Answer: B
Rationale: Text B identifies "poor sanitation" and "Lack of breastfeeding in infancy removes a key source 
of passive immunity" as environmental risk factors.
Question 7.   Answer: A
Rationale: Text A: "Dehydration is the most serious complication of diarrhea and is classified as mild, 
moderate, or severe based on clinical signs." This phrasing is specific to Text A.
Questions 8–14
Question 8.   Answer: three (3)
Rationale: "Diarrhea is defined as the passage of three or more loose or watery stools within a 24-hour 
period." (Text A, paragraph 1)
Question 9.   Answer: rotavirus
Rationale: "Rotavirus remains the most common viral cause globally." (Text A, paragraph 3)
Question 10.   Answer: two (2)
Rationale: "children under two years of age being at greatest risk" (Text B, paragraph 2)
Question 11.   Answer: duration and severity
Rationale: "Zinc supplementation given alongside ORS has been shown to reduce the duration and 
severity of acute diarrheal episodes." (Text B, paragraph 5)
Question 12.   Answer: persistent
Rationale: "Persistent diarrhea is defined as an episode lasting 14 days or more but fewer than 30 days." 
(Text A, paragraph 2)
Question 13.   Answer: intravenous
Rationale: "Severe dehydration requires prompt intravenous fluid resuscitation (Plan C)." (Text D, Step 3)
Question 14.   Answer: intestinal integrity
Rationale: "Continued feeding maintains intestinal integrity, supports recovery, and prevents malnutrition." 
(Text D, Step 4)

Questions 15–20
Question 15.   Answer: sunken eyes
Rationale: Text C table: "Sunken eyes and dry mucous membranes" listed as clinical feature indicating 
moderate dehydration.
Question 16.   Answer: oral rehydration solution (ORS)
Rationale: Text B: "Oral rehydration solution (ORS) administration at the onset of diarrhea prevents 
progression to significant dehydration."
Question 17.   Answer: hypovolemic shock
Rationale: Text A: "Continued fluid and nutritional loss without adequate replacement may lead to 
hypovolemic shock." Also referenced in Text C note.
Question 18.   Answer: exclusive breastfeeding
Rationale: Text B: "Exclusive breastfeeding for the first six months of life is strongly recommended and 
significantly reduces diarrheal morbidity."
Question 19.   Answer: nutritional assessment
Rationale: Text C table: "Persistent diarrhea exceeding 14 days | Risk of malnutrition and micronutrient 
deficiency | Conduct nutritional assessment and refer to specialist."
Question 20.   Answer: warning signs / when to seek further medical attention
Rationale: Text D, Step 5: "Caregivers should be educated on recognising warning signs and when to 
seek further medical attention."
